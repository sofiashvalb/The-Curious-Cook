My version of tomato soup isn’t traditional, but trust me, it’s rich, vibrant, and totally worth the try! ✨
I like it light with croutons, but my husband prefers it with rice—he's all about that extra comfort! 🍲

Ingredients:

Vegetables:

  • 🍅 4-5 ripe tomatoes
  • 🧅 1 onion
  • 🌿 2 celery stalks
  • 🥕 1 carrot
  • 🥒 1 zucchini
  • 🌶️ 1 bell pepper
  • 🧄 3-4 garlic cloves

Herbs & Roots:

  • 🌱 Fresh parsley, cilantro, and celery leaves (optional: parsley root, celery root)
  • 🌶️ Fresh ginger (optional)

Liquids & Seasonings:

  • 🍅 1 can of tomato paste
  • 🧂 Salt & black pepper to taste
  • 🍯 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • 🍲 1 cup vegetable broth (optional but adds richness)
  • 💧 3-4 cups water (adjust based on desired consistency)

Optional Extras:

  • 🍞 Croutons
  • 🍚 Rice
  • 🥄 Sour cream
Tips & Suggestions:
  • ✨ You can add/remove any vegetables you want. Every vegetable has it's own magic and flavor.
  • 💡 For a lighter, flavorful soup, I like to add croutons and sour cream. If you want something heartier, try adding rice—just cook it separately and stir it in right before serving!
Let's Do This:
  • In a pot, sauté onion, celery, carrot, and any root vegetables (if using) in a little oil. Once the veggies are tender, add minced garlic, bell pepper and zucchini. Stir for a minute, then toss in the chopped tomatoes.
  • Let the tomatoes break down, then add parsley, cilantro, and celery leaves. Season with salt, pepper, and sugar (optional).
  • Stir in the tomato paste, vegetable broth, and water. Cover, and let it simmer for about an hour on medium heat. Check occasionally to make sure there's enough liquid.
  • Once the veggies are soft and fragrant, blend with a blender until smooth (adjust consistency with water if needed).
  • Ladle into bowls and top with a spoonful of rice or croutons. You can add a spoon of sour cream. Enjoy the rich, savory flavors! 🍲🍞
